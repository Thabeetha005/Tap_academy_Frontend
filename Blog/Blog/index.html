Inside Out 2:

•    Title: Inside Out 2
•    Release Year: 2024
•    Genre: Animation, Family, Comedy, Drama
•    Studio: Pixar Animation Studios
•    Distributor: Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures
•    Director: Kelsey Mann
•    Producer: Mark Nielsen
•    Language: English
•    Runtime: ~96 minutes
•    Description:  Pixar animated sequel that continues the story of Riley as she enters her teenage years, bringing major changes inside her mind.
Along with Joy, Sadness, Anger, Fear, and Disgust, new emotions such as Anxiety, Envy, Embarrassment, and Ennui arrive, creating chaos in Headquarters
and changing how Riley thinks and reacts to the world around her. The film explores themes of growing up, peer pressure, self-identity, and mental health 
in a simple and relatable way, blending humor with emotional moments. With stunning animation and meaningful storytelling, Inside Out 2 connects with both 
teens and adults, showing that every emotion—good or bad—plays an important role in personal growth.


Soul:

•   Title: Soul
•   Release Year: 2020
•   Genre: Animation, Family, Comedy, Fantasy, Drama
•   Studio: Pixar Animation Studios
•   Distributor: Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures
•   Director: Pete Docter
•   Producer: Dana Murray
•   Language: English
•   Runtime: ~100 minutes
•   Description:  Pixar animated film directed by Pete Docter that follows Joe Gardner, a middle-school band teacher who dreams of becoming a successful jazz musician. 
When he finally gets the opportunity to perform professionally, an unexpected accident separates his soul from his body and sends him to the Great Before, a colorful world 
where souls develop their personalities before being born on Earth. Desperate to return to his life, Joe teams up with a soul named 22, who has no interest in living, and 
together they begin a journey that changes how both of them see life. The film beautifully explores themes of purpose, passion, identity, and the meaning of life, showing that 
life’s true value is found not only in big achievements but also in small, everyday moments. With stunning animation, emotional storytelling, and a powerful jazz-inspired soundtrack, 
Soul connects deeply with both adults and younger audiences, making it one of Pixar’s most thoughtful and meaningful films.
